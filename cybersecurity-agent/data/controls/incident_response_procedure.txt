INCIDENT RESPONSE PROCEDURE
Version: 2.0 | Effective: 2024-06-01 | Owner: Security Operations

1. PURPOSE
Define the steps the organisation follows when a security incident is detected to
minimise impact and restore normal operations promptly.

2. INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION
- Severity 1 (Critical): Data breach, ransomware, complete system outage.
- Severity 2 (High): Malware infection, unauthorised privilege escalation.
- Severity 3 (Medium): Phishing attempt, policy violation, suspicious activity.

3. RESPONSE PHASES

Phase 1 — Detection & Identification
Security alerts are monitored by the IT team during business hours (09:00–18:00).
Alerts outside business hours are reviewed the following morning. Incidents are
logged in the IT helpdesk ticketing system.

Phase 2 — Containment
The affected system is isolated from the network. Temporary access controls are
applied to limit lateral movement.

Phase 3 — Eradication
Malicious artefacts are removed. Systems are patched and hardened before
restoration.

Phase 4 — Recovery
Systems are restored from the most recent clean backup. Business stakeholders are
notified once systems are operational.

4. NOTED DEFICIENCIES
- No 24/7 on-call rotation; critical incidents outside business hours go undetected.
- No formal communication plan: regulators, customers, and legal counsel are not
  notified within required timeframes.
- Post-incident reviews (lessons learned) are performed informally and findings
  are not tracked or fed back into security controls.
- Incident response runbooks exist only for ransomware; other attack types lack
  documented procedures.
